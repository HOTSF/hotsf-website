The Crisis

A Craft at a Crossroads

American watchmaking faces a critical workforce shortage. The number of practicing watchmakers in the US has plummeted from approximately 44,000 in 1953 to fewer than 2,000 today. With a 10:1 hiring ratio — ten positions for every qualified watchmaker — the industry desperately needs new talent.

Yet only 9 accredited full-time watchmaking schools exist in the entire country, with a combined capacity of just 42 students per year. At the Rolex Dallas campus alone, 560 applicants compete for only 27 spots — a 4.82% acceptance rate.

Scholarships

Opening Doors for Aspiring Watchmakers

Our scholarship program awards $2,500–$5,000 to students enrolled in accredited watchmaking programs across the United States. The first scholarships are planned for 2027, with applications opening late 2026.

We believe that financial need should never stand between a dedicated student and a career in horology.
